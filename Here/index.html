Here Is My Place

Turning Warsaw to healthy and vibrant city using maps


Find out why and how?

Challenge

Warsaw, a capital of Poland, is attracting people with lots of working opportunities. Almost 300K of them commute every day from surrounding areas to their place of work spending spending on average 41 minutes in a seat. Huge amount of car traffic makes the city by far too congested and inaccessible for citizens. Moreover, with rapidly expanding high building business and housing ares, the air quality seems to be the most crucial issue that local authorities have to tackle as a first priority.

So... how can maps and HERE XYZ services can help to decrease congestion and improve the quality of air? Simply, invest more in the eco-friendly infrastructure, encourage people to change their habits by showing them what profits they could have achieved. Here is how to do that:

Step #1

Vast areas of Greenery

Although Varsovians might enjoy huge areas of parks (~ 110 m2/person which stands the city at the 12th place around the world) at stone's throw, there is still a lot to be done.

The spatial distribution of green areas does not overlay with locations of the most populated areas (See map below). The lack of natural areas is especially affecting the city center (Śródmieście) and the south-west part of the city (Ursus) depicted as red areas on the map below. Thus, some more natural areas needs to be created there to let people unwind and help city to breath a cleaner air.

Step #2

Kilometers of Cycleways

Build more cycling routes connecting most populated areas with business districts. According to the latest data, everyday there is around 75K cyclists travelling across 600 kilometers of available cycleways.

This is worth-spreading solution as it encourages citizens to take-up healthier life-style, reduces emission on CO2 / NOX and helps to save some money.
